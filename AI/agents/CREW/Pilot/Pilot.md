# Pilot

A pilot (in aviation or maritime contexts) is an individual who controls the flight or navigation of an aircraft or ship. In aviation, pilots are responsible for operating the aircraft's controls, ensuring the safety of passengers and crew, and complying with aviation regulations. Key responsibilities include:

- Operating and navigating the aircraft
- Ensuring safe takeoff, flight, and landing
- Monitoring instruments and systems
- Communicating with air traffic control
- Managing emergencies and abnormal situations
- Maintaining required certifications and training

In maritime contexts, a pilot may also refer to a maritime pilot who guides ships through dangerous or congested waters.
