Traveller RPG Starship Squadrons: Research Summary

Overview & Canonical Sources

  • "Squadron" = military formation of starships, part of a navy or spacefaring force.
  • Key sources: Classic Traveller Book 5: High Guard, Supplement 9: Fighting Ships, Spinward Marches Campaign, GURPS Traveller: Spinward Marches, Mongoose Traveller: Spinward Marches, and others.

Typical Squadron Composition

  • Battle Squadrons: Battleships or heavy cruisers
  • Cruiser Squadrons: 3–6 cruisers
  • Destroyer/Frigate Squadrons: 4–8 smaller warships
  • Escort/Patrol Squadrons: 6–12 escorts, scouts, or SDBs
  • Grouped into flotillas, task forces, fleets; varies by navy and era

Notable Squadrons in the Spinward Marches

  • Imperial Navy’s 212th Battle Squadron (Regina)
  • 43rd Cruiser Squadron (Efate)
  • 4518th Lift Infantry Regiment’s transport squadron
  • Zhodani and Sword Worlds squadrons (varied doctrines)
  • See Supplement 9: Fighting Ships for more examples

Data & Conventions for Representing Squadrons

  • Sector/world data rarely encodes squadrons directly; look for naval bases, homeports, garrisons
  • Squadron data: Name/Number, Homeport/Base, Ship Types/Classes, Affiliation, sometimes CO
  • Community resources: TravellerMap.com, Citizens of the Imperium forums, T5SS data

Expanded Squadron Lists & Orders of Battle

  • Imperial Navy (Spinward Marches): 212th Battle Squadron (Regina), 43rd Cruiser Squadron (Efate), 4518th Lift Infantry Regiment’s transport squadron, 17th and 23rd Patrol Squadrons (various bases). See Imperial Navy and Spinward Marches sources.
  • Zhodani Consulate: 1st, 2nd, and 3rd Zhodani Squadrons (Jewell, Arden, and other border worlds), with detailed ship classes in Zhodani Navy Fleet Composition.
  • Sword Worlds: Gram Fleet, Sacnoth Fleet, Narsil Fleet, Joyeuse Fleet, with squadrons often named for their homeworlds or legendary swords. See Sword Worlds Confederation.
  • Vargr Extents: Squadrons and bands are highly variable, often named after charismatic leaders or pack traditions. Examples: Arrghonz Dreadnought Squadron, Rrazaaghz Corsair Bands. See Vargr Navy.

Squadron Sizes, Ship Classes, and Doctrines

  • Imperium: Squadrons typically 3–8 capital ships (battleships, cruisers), or 6–12 escorts. Doctrines emphasize layered defense, rapid response, and sector fleet integration.
  • Zhodani: Squadrons often include battle riders, carriers, and psionic support ships. Doctrines focus on telepathic coordination and deep penetration raids.
  • Sword Worlds: Squadrons are smaller, with a mix of locally-built cruisers, battle riders, and escorts. Emphasis on aggressive tactics and homeworld defense.
  • Vargr: Organization is fluid; "bands" may be 2–12 ships, sometimes dozens. Charisma-based leadership, with squadrons forming and dissolving rapidly. Mix of corsairs, raiders, and captured ships.

Notable Historical Battles & Campaigns

  • Fourth Frontier War: Battle of Two Suns (Imperial 212th vs. Zhodani squadrons).
  • Fifth Frontier War: Major fleet actions in Regina, Jewell, and Efate subsectors; Sword Worlds and Vargr squadrons as allies or raiders.
  • Other: Numerous local conflicts, especially in the Spinward Marches and Vargr border sectors.

Community & Fan Data Representations

  • TravellerMap.com: Sector/world data, naval base locations, and some OOBs.
  • Traveller Wiki: Detailed ship class pages, squadron lists, and campaign summaries.
  • Citizens of the Imperium Forums: User-created OOBs, campaign logs, and alternate histories.
  • T5SS Data: Sector surveys, world data, and some fleet assignments in tabular format.

Data Formats, Tables, and OOBs

  • Squadron OOBs are often presented as tables: Name, Base, Ship Types, Affiliation, CO, Source.
  • Fan projects may use CSV, Markdown, or Wiki tables. Example: 
    | Squadron Name | Base | Ships | Affiliation | Source |
|---------------|------|-------|-------------|--------|
| 212th Battle  | Regina | 4 BB, 2 BC | Imperial | Spinward Marches |

  • TravellerMap and T5SS provide downloadable sector/world data in tabular and JSON formats.
